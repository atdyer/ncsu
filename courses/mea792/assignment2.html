Motivation

The goal of this flight, is to collect aerial imagery of the FRF in Duck, NC so that imagery processing techniques can be compared with LIDAR measurement techniques in a coastal setting. We hope to be able to determine if beach volume can be accurately calculated using the DSMs generated using aerial imagery, thus providing a much more cost effective way of studying the evolution of our beaches as compared to current LIDAR techniques.

Flight Planning

The following images show the target area, projected flight plan, and take-off and landing locations for the Trimble UX5 flight:

The flight area is private government property, owned by the US Army Corps of Engineers (permission granted through research funding). The only obstacles in the area are a small gazebo in a parking lot off the side of the main road, the main FRF building as well as two garages, and low shrubs and bushes. While the area does not currently have a COA, aeronautical charts show that the property is not located in any restricted airspace.

The FRF property is approximate 1km alongshore and 0.6km wide, giving an area of 0.6km2. However, the most important imagery to obtain is the beach itself. Ideally, I'd like to plan a mission that flies very low over the beach in order to obtain higher resolution imagery (on the order of millimeters/pixel) in order to compare with the extremely high resolution LIDAR data we collect.
